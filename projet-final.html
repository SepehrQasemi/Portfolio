ATA CRM - Final Project

Design and delivery of a SaaS CRM for a B2B company trading food products and additives.

Open Live Demo Open CRM Repository Open Deliverables Hub

Business Scenario

Context used for implementation and defense

ATA CRM supports a food ingredients trading company managing supplier relationships, customer follow-up, negotiated quotes, and conversion forecasting. The platform centralizes companies, contacts, products, leads, tasks, emails, and role-based collaboration.

Architecture and Modules

Implementation stack and core capabilities

Stack

Next.js 16, Supabase (PostgreSQL + Auth + RLS), Brevo, Vercel, GitHub Actions.

Sales Operations

Leads pipeline, stage transitions, collaboration tracking, weighted forecast, and task planning.

Data Modules

Companies, contacts, products, and categories with profile pages and relationship views.

Email Module

Manual send, role-aware analytics, templates, and protected automation jobs for admin.

Access Model

Admin, manager, commercial, and standard user with role-scoped behavior and protected actions.

Quality Layer

Unit/API/E2E checks, CI gates, and documented compliance mapping against project requirements.

CRM Workflow and Pipeline

Operational journey from first contact to closure
  • Pipeline stages: New Lead, Qualification, Sample Sent, Quote Sent, Negotiation, Won, Lost.
  • Negotiation has explicit decision actions (Mark Won / Mark Lost).
  • Quick actions include previous stage, edit, and task creation from lead cards.
  • Weighted forecasting uses stage probability progression to estimate short-term revenue.

Live Demo Flow (Instructor Walkthrough)

Compact path replacing the previous presentation page
  1. Sign in and verify role-specific navigation.
  2. Create or open a company profile, then attach relevant products.
  3. Open a contact profile linked to the target company.
  4. Create a new lead with fixed source and assignment behavior.
  5. Move lead stages up to negotiation, then close as won or lost.
  6. Create follow-up tasks and show calendar impact.
  7. Send a manual email and review scoped analytics.
  8. Open dashboard KPI and weighted forecast indicators.
  9. Show documentation proof from the deliverables links.
